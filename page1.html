Who is Linus Torvalds?

Linus Torvalds (BIOGRAPHY) and the Birth of Linux (The Origin)

Linus Torvalds (born December 28, 1969, in Helsinki Finland) is a Finnish/American software engineer best known as the creator of the Linux kernel. Raised in an academic family, he discovered programming as a teenager and developed a passion for understanding how computers work. While studying computer science at the University of Helsinki, Torvalds began working on a personal project to explore the Intel 386 processor and build a free Unix-like environment for himself. This hobby project became Linux, first released in 1991.
The Goal

Linus Torvalds’ initial goal in 1991 was simply to create a small, free, Unix-like system he could use on his own PC. He wanted to learn the Intel 386 processor in depth and build a better environment than MINIX, which he found limiting. At the time, he saw Linux purely as a personal hobby project, something to experiment with and improve for his own use, not a future global operating system.
Security

When people say Linux is “more secure” than Windows, they often refer to differences in how each system handles code design and user permissions. Linux is open source, meaning its code is publicly visible. Thousands of developers and security experts constantly review it, bugs and vulnerabilities are often found and fixed quickly because many eyes inspect the code. Windows is closed source, so only Microsoft can review or modify most of its internal code, this means fewer people can inspect it for vulnerabilities.
